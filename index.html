  1. Main Point 1
    • Cyber security is a growing concern over the past decades and is something that is considered a national effort to practice safety.
      1. Cyber security can be defined as the practice of ensuring the integrability, confidentiality, and availability of information, a definition given by J.M. Porup, the Senior Writer at CSO, a news article dedicated at serving security decision-makers with the critical information they need to stay ahead of evolving threats and defense against criminal cyberattacks.
      2. Staying ahead of cybercrime is an issue that all nations face, including the United States. The aftermath of eight major cyber attacks in 2016, including one on the U.S. Department of Justice in which over twenty thousand FBI employee’s information got leaked, caused the National Security Agency’s deputy national manager for national security systems, Curt Dukes, to speak on the issue. On October 16th, at the American Enterprise Institute, Dukes said that “…as a nation we have to rethink how we’re actually organized when we do cyber defense to protect the whole of the nation…” In the DoD (Department of Defense) news article written by Amaani Lyle, Dukes mentions the length of time it takes to react to a cybercrime and that the system in place takes far too long to stop the hacker’s objective.

  2. Main Point 2
    • So, what is a computer virus? According to the Symantec Corporation, the world’s leading cyber security company supporting more than fifty million families via their Norton Security platform, it is “…much like a flu virus, (it) is designed to spread from host to host and has the ability to replicate itself. Similarly, in the same way that flu viruses cannot reproduce without a host cell, computer viruses cannot reproduce and spread without programming such as a file or document.” Just like the flu there are multiple kinds and variations of viruses.
      1. According to Dell’s customer support team, Dell being a global leading tech company, viruses can be categorized into nine different classes. The most common two being malware and adware. Malware is any type of software that can affect anyone’s device negatively and can gather a user’s personal data. Adware is a software that randomly causes advertisements to pop up on to the user’s screen to generate revenue for its original owner. Having advertisements pop up on the screen does not sound so bad since we are so used to it happening whenever we visit a website or using an app, so how bad can a virus actually be?
      2. Computer viruses clearly do not get the same sickness as we do. Their definition of a “cold” is slow performance, system crashing, and the losing of files. Also, just like us, viruses are not the only sickness for them. They can get computer worms, which essentially eat up all the bandwidth data making your internet seem really slow. There are these things called Trojan horses, which just like in the history books just secretly wait inside your device until the right moment comes to steal your passwords. Lastly there is spyware, which is like a worse version of adware, it still causing advertisements to pop up but now it has the ability to steal your credit card information.
